MG — Mammographic Risk Foundation Model
An imaging-only breast cancer risk model trained on ~40,000 institutional screening exams (~469 cancer-positive patients) at Einstein/Montefiore Medical Center. Architecture: EfficientNetV2S backbone + ViewTransformer (CLS token) exam-level aggregation + discrete hazard head for 1–5 year cumulative risk prediction. Achieves mean validation AUC 0.793 across 1–4 year horizons using pixel features only — no clinical variables (no family history, hormonal status, or prior biopsy). Performance is comparable to Mirai’s published ~0.76 mean AUC, demonstrating that imaging features alone are sufficient for competitive risk discrimination when a sufficiently expressive cross-view aggregation architecture is used. Designed as an extensible foundation for longitudinal and tomosynthesis-based breast cancer risk prediction. External validation at Columbia University Medical Center: ongoing.

0 — Dataset & Filtering

Cohort: All 2D digital mammography examinations performed at Einstein/Montefiore Medical Center and affiliated satellite sites. DICOM images were converted to PNG using dcm2img with min-max window normalisation, followed by threshold-based breast crop. Examinations were linked to the institutional cancer registry to identify patients with subsequent breast cancer diagnoses within 5 years. A minimum follow-up of 12 months was required for inclusion in the training cohort. Risk-head training required complete 4-view acquisition (LCC, LMLO, RCC, RMLO) and known cancer-registry status. Patients were stratified by cancer status and randomly assigned to training (80%) or validation (20%) sets; no patient contributed to both sets.
Starting from all ingested 2D mammograms, exams pass through a series of eligibility filters that determine which tasks each exam contributes to. Exams are never fully excluded — they contribute to the tasks for which they have sufficient data.

2 — Pipeline Overview

2.1 — Model Architecture

View Encoder (effv2s): Each of the 4 standard mammogram views (LCC, LMLO, RCC, RMLO) is encoded independently by an EfficientNetV2S backbone pretrained on ImageNet. The input convolution was adapted from 3-channel RGB to single-channel grayscale by averaging pretrained weights across the channel dimension, preserving ImageNet representations. The backbone produces a 1280-dim feature vector per view; weights are shared across all views.
View Aggregation — ViewTransformer: The 4 per-view feature vectors are treated as a token sequence with learnable view-type positional embeddings (one per view: LCC, LMLO, RCC, RMLO). A CLS token is prepended and the sequence is passed through a 2-layer, 8-head Transformer encoder (pre-norm variant, inner dim 2×1280, dropout 0.1). The CLS token output provides the exam-level representation. This replaces the masked mean-pooling used in Mirai, allowing the model to capture bilateral asymmetry and cross-view correlations that are averaged away by pooling. Padding masks handle exams with fewer than 4 views.

2.2 — Data Flow

B exams × 4 views → view encoder → B×4 feature vectors → reshape to sequences → Transformer → B exam embeddings → task heads.
Aux heads receive the per-view features before pooling. Risk head receives the exam-level pooled embedding. Images are normalised per-image to zero mean / unit variance before encoding.

2.3 — Patient-Level Data Split

No patient leakage: all exams from the same patient are assigned to the same train/val fold. Stratification ensures comparable cancer-positive rates across folds. Multiple exams per patient are treated independently at training time but share fold membership.

2.4 — Staged Curriculum Learning

Phase 1 — Auxiliary Pretrain (epochs 0–5): Auxiliary and regression tasks only; risk head weight = 0. Goal: initialise the backbone with clinically meaningful low-level features (view geometry, exposure parameters, patient age) before the risk signal is introduced. Prevents the risk head from corrupting the backbone with noisy early gradients. The EfficientNetV2S backbone is additionally frozen for the first freeze_backbone_epochs (spanning pretrain and early taper) to preserve ImageNet representations.
Phase 2 — Taper (epochs 5–15): Aux weight linearly annealed 1.0→0.25; risk weight ramped 0.0→1.0 over 10 epochs. Smooth transition avoids the instability of abruptly switching objectives.
Phase 3 — Risk Training (epoch 15+): Risk head dominates (weight = 1.0). Auxiliary heads retained at weight 0.25 as regularisation — they prevent the encoder from over-fitting to the risk label distribution and maintain view-level feature quality. The best checkpoint is selected by highest mean validation AUC across horizons 1–4 years (5-year data excluded from checkpoint selection due to near-total cancer prevalence in follow-up-eligible exams).

2.6 — Discrete Hazard Formulation

Per-interval hazard rates $h_t = \sigma(z_t)$ composed into cumulative risk:
$$F(t) = 1 - \prod_{k=1}^{t}(1 - h_k)$$
Cumulative risk within $t$ years
Loss: BCE at each observable interval $t \in \mathcal{M}$: $$\mathcal{L} = -\frac{1}{|\mathcal{M}|} \sum_{t \in \mathcal{M}} \Big[ y_t \log h_t + (1-y_t)\log(1-h_t)\Big]$$

3 — Training Curves

The three curriculum phases are visible in the loss and AUC curves. Phase 1 (auxiliary pretrain): risk AUC is near chance (0.5) — the risk head has not yet been activated. Phase 2 (taper): a sharp improvement in validation AUC as the risk head receives increasing gradient weight; risk loss falls rapidly as the hazard head learns from the training signal. Phase 3 (risk-dominant): continued improvement with the backbone adapting to the risk objective. The best checkpoint is selected at the epoch with the highest mean 1–4yr validation AUC.
